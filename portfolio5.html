Organizational Development and Change Management Plan

Original file: Download DOCX

GreenSpark was designed as a sustainability consulting microbusiness focused on helping organizations improve environmental performance through training, workshops, and strategic advisory services. However, offering sustainable services is not enough. For GreenSpark to remain credible and effective, sustainability must also be embedded internally in how employees collaborate, make decisions, and respond to change.

Part I: Organizational Development Plan

Organizational Development (OD) is a planned, systematic process for improving organizational effectiveness by aligning people, structures, and processes with strategic goals. In sustainability-driven organizations like GreenSpark, OD ensures environmental values are not symbolic but operational.

Part II: Change Management Plan (ADKAR Model)

For GreenSpark, the most appropriate change management framework is the ADKAR Model. ADKAR stands for Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, and Reinforcement. Unlike large-scale transformation models, ADKAR focuses on individual behavioral change, making it especially effective for small, mission-driven businesses.

GreenSpark applies ADKAR by communicating sustainability urgency, involving employees in decisions, providing practical training, supplying implementation tools, and reinforcing progress through sustainability-centered performance measures.

The portfolio concludes that sustainable transformation does not happen by accident. It requires intentional design, behavioral alignment, and structured reinforcement.
